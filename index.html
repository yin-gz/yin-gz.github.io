About Me

I am a postdoctoral assistant researcher at School of Computing, Harbin Institute of Technology (HIT). I received my Ph.D. from HIT in September 2025, under the supervision of Prof. Hongli Zhang.

My research mainly focuses on computational social network analysis and safety assurance. It spans three related sub-areas: online cognitive security, social network analysis, online information content analysis. I develop computational methods for modeling content, actors, relations, and influence in online social networks.

Research Interests

Online Cognitive Security

Computational modeling of online user cognition and collective cognition, including cognitive simulation, online cognitive behavior analysis, and cognitive influence assessment in networked environments.

Agent-Based Simulation AI for Social Science Knowledge Reasoning Computational Cognitive Science Proactive Social Agent

Social Network Analysis

Modeling users, groups, organizations, and their relations in complex social systems for link prediction, influence analysis, and abnormal behavior detection.

Graph Neural Networks Knowledge Graphs Link Prediction Hypergraph Reasoning

Online Information Content Analysis

Detection, understanding, and governance of risky online content, including misinformation, rumors, harmful information, stance signals, and coordinated social spam.

Multimodal Learning Misinformation Detection Risk Prediction
